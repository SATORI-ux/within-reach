# Product Brief

## Product
**Within Reach** is a private two-person check-in app accessed through personal NFC tiles.

It is a shared space for quiet presence.

It is not built to maximize conversation volume, engagement, or feature richness.
It exists to support small acts of return.

---

## Core thesis
This product should feel like:
- a handcrafted object translated into a webpage
- a small ritual
- a place to leave traces
- a place to quietly return to

It should not feel like:
- a messenger
- a feed product
- a dashboard
- a social platform
- a productivity surface

The emotional goal is presence, not novelty.

---

## Product structure
The experience has three layers:

1. **Arrival**
2. **Signal**
3. **Record**

This structure should remain stable.

### Arrival
Arrival is a brief personal landing moment after tile access.

Purpose:
- recognition
- emotional transition
- a soft pause before the shared space opens

### Signal
Signal is the interaction center of the app.

The three actions are:

- `Thinking of you`
- `Leave a note`
- `Can you talk?`

Each action has a distinct role and that distinction matters.

### Record
The record is split into two different surfaces:

- **Check-ins**: ambient, light, templated, no reactions
- **Notes**: deeper, readable, chronological, reaction-enabled

Do not collapse these into a single mixed stream.

---

## Action meanings

### Thinking of you
This is the emotional center of the product.

It should feel:
- low-friction
- ambient
- memorable
- gentle
- meaningful without becoming heavy

It is the closest behavior to a friendship lamp.

The user should be able to send it almost without thinking.

### Leave a note
This is the slower surface.

It should feel:
- reflective
- personal
- guestbook-like
- calm
- intentional

It is not chat.
It is not back-and-forth messaging.
It is the deeper shared record.

### Can you talk?
This is the serious path.

It should feel:
- calm
- distinct
- private
- human
- more important than a normal check-in

It should not feel:
- alarming
- theatrical
- like an emergency app
- like public history

Urgent events remain private in backend flow and are not rendered in the visible shared record.

---

## What the product must preserve
Always preserve these product truths:

- quietness over novelty
- clarity over abundance
- emotional coherence over capability
- smallness over accumulation
- object-feel over app-feel
- two-person intimacy over generalization

If a proposed change makes the app feel more like software and less like an object, it is likely the wrong direction.

---

## Current product risks
These are the most likely ways the product can drift:

### 1. Feed growth
If the page grows indefinitely, it starts reading like an archive instead of a held place.

Preferred approach:
- recent items by default
- quiet reveal of older history
- no heavy numeric pagination
- no boxed mini-scroll regions unless unavoidable

### 2. Interaction drift
Adding more actions, states, or visible controls can weaken the original emotional split.

Protect the meaning of the three existing actions.

### 3. Tone drift
Generic product copy, visible system logic, or loud urgency can damage trust faster than missing features.

### 4. Boundary drift
When private logic moves into client-only behavior, the product becomes easier to manipulate and easier to accidentally expose.

---

## Product success bar
The product is succeeding when:

- arrival feels like recognition
- quick actions feel emotionally distinct
- check-ins feel light
- notes feel calm and readable
- urgent remains private and serious
- the page feels small even as it accumulates history
- the user experiences the app as a place, not a tool

---

## Default decision rule
When unclear, choose:
- less
- quieter
- smaller
- more legible
- more bounded
- more emotionally coherent
